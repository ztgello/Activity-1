How to Start a Video Content?

The step by step guide.

STEP 2

Equipments. It doesn't really have to be an expensive equipment as long as it does the work, with our help you will be able to get the most out of the ones you have.


STEP 3

It's finally time to prepare setting up your pc, preparing the camera (optional) setting up your hardware properly and downloading the right softwares such as OBS, Premiere pro and etc and most importantly the content that you might want to start wether it might be a game, narration, tutorial or etc.


STEP 4

Essential softwares: OBS (the recording software) Any video editing software but, we do encourage everyone to use premiere pro as it is becoming the golden standard.


STEP 5

OBS settings for a more optimized recording without any framedrops, as well as how to record.

Your ideal OBS setup will depend on your CPU and GPU. A wise starting point would be to consult the Auto-Configuration Wizard. You can find it in the Tools menu.

Based on your system and hardware, it will suggest the most stable OBS settings for recording. So, if you have a higher-end processor and graphics card, you’ll be able to push for higher video and audio settings. Otherwise, you’ll have to make do with lower and mid-level options. With that in mind, let’s get into it. you’ll mainly focus on two sections: Video and Output. First, make your way to the Settings. You can find them in the lower right corner of the interface. Alternatively, go to the File tab in the top left corner.

moving forward your next stop will be on the video section highlighted below, this setting can really maximize the recording with a cause of quality. However, if your pc can handle a bit more you can go to the standard resolution of 1920x1080 the top left corner.

The Recording Path is where your clips will be saved. the OBS Recording Format field is important. If you choose MP4/MOV, do keep in mind that your video will be unrecoverable in case of a power outage, BSOD, or if OBS crashes. You’re better off with MKV or FLV if that's an issue. Your choice of Encoder is another big decision. Your GPU and CPU will mainly influence it. If you have an NVidia Series graphics card, you should choose NVENC. With a mid-range CPU and AMD graphics card, go with AMF. If you have a good processor, you’re best off with x264. Either way, new options will appear.

STEP 6

You're all set and ready to go!
